The Morning Listen Project

Urban Bird Sound Recording Volunteer Protocol – Durham, NC | Spring 2025

Overview Instructions Equipment Schedule Questions

Overview of the Project

The Morning Listen Project is a community science initiative investigating how urban environments shape bird diversity. Volunteers will help us collect 5-minute bird sound recordings at random locations in Durham using the Merlin Bird ID app. This data will help us understand patterns like the "luxury effect," where wealthier neighborhoods sometimes—but not always—host greater biodiversity. Your participation is key to uncovering what’s really going on!

Step-by-Step Instructions for Volunteers

1. Choose Your Location(s)

  • Choose from 35 randomized sampling points across Durham (see map).
  • Try to be within 50 yards of the designated location. All points are in publicly accessible areas.

2. Recordings

  • Visit your site 4 times between May and June 2025.
  • Record within 2 hours of sunrise (between 6:22–5:59 AM across the season).
  • Use the Merlin app: select “Sound ID,” record for 5 minutes, then email the file to Bradley.Allf@colostate.edu.

3. Note Conditions

  • Include a short description of the soundscape and any noise (traffic, lawnmowers, etc.).
  • Mention the species Merlin detected and weather conditions.

4. Be Observant

  • If approached, explain the project politely. If asked to leave, do so and notify us.

5. Movement During Recording

  • You can move gently but avoid making noise or leaving the immediate area.

Equipment Needed

  • Smartphone
  • Merlin Bird ID app (free download)
  • Internet to email recordings
  • Optional: Volunteer hats or badges via New Hope Bird Alliance

Schedule Overview
